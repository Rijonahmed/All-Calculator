Before you start, gather these tools:

- **Laser measure** — Most accurate, works alone

## Step-by-Step: How to Measure a Rectangle Room

This is the most common room shape:

1. **Measure Length:** Longest wall, from corner to corner
2. **Measure Width:** Shorter wall, from corner to corner
3. **Calculate Area:** Length × Width

**Example:** A room that's 14 feet long × 12 feet wide

> 14 × 12 = **168 square feet**

## Measuring Irregular Rooms

### L-Shaped Rooms

Divide into two rectangles:

1. Measure Rectangle A (length × width)
2. Measure Rectangle B (length × width)
3. Add both areas

### Rooms with Alcoves or Bay Windows

- Calculate the main room area first
- Calculate the alcove separately
- Add them together for the total

### Circular or Curved Areas

- For half-circles: (π × r²) ÷ 2
- For quarter-circles: (π × r²) ÷ 4

## Unit Conversions Made Easy

| Common Conversion | Formula |
|-------------------|---------|
| Square Feet → Square Meters | ÷ 10.764 |
| Square Meters → Square Feet | × 10.764 |
| Square Inches → Square Feet | ÷ 144 |
| Square Yards → Square Feet | × 9 |

## Real-World Examples

### Flooring a Room
Room: 14 ft × 12 ft = 168 sq ft
Add 10% waste: 168 × 1.10 = **~185 sq ft of flooring needed**

### Painting Walls
Room: 14 ft × 12 ft with 8 ft ceiling
Wall area = 2 × (14 × 8) + 2 × (12 × 8) = 224 + 192 = **416 sq ft**
Subtract doors (~20 sq ft each) and windows (~15 sq ft each)

### Carpet Installation
Room: 5 m × 4 m = 20 m²
Carpet is sold in sq ft: 20 × 10.764 = **~215 sq ft**

## Common Measurement Mistakes

- **Measuring only once** — always measure twice
- **Forgetting to subtract doors and windows** for paint calculation
- **Using outside dimensions** instead of interior wall-to-wall
- **Not accounting for closets** in total square footage
- **Rounding too early** — keep decimals until the final result

## Why Calculate Truck Capacity?

Whether you're a construction professional, landscaper, or homeowner ordering materials, knowing your truck's exact capacity is crucial. Under-ordering means multiple trips and wasted time. Over-ordering wastes money and storage space.

Our [**Truck Calculator**](/truck-calculator) helps you measure exactly how many cubic feet (CFT) your dump truck can carry.

## How the Calculator Works

The formula is simple: **Volume (CFT) = Length × Width × Height**

1. **Measure Your Truck Bed**: Use a measuring tape to find the inner dimensions
2. **Enter Dimensions**: Input length, width, and height in feet and inches
3. **Get Results**: The calculator instantly shows total volume in cubic feet

## Understanding CFT (Cubic Feet)

Cubic feet is the standard unit for measuring truck capacity. One cubic foot is:

- A cube that is 1 foot × 1 foot × 1 foot
- Approximately 28.3 liters
- About 7.5 gallons

## Typical Truck Capacities

| Truck Type | Approx. CFT | Best For |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| Small Pickup | 40-60 CFT | Small home projects |
| Full-size Pickup | 60-100 CFT | Medium landscaping |
| Dump Truck (small) | 200-400 CFT | Construction sites |
| Dump Truck (large) | 400-800+ CFT | Major earth moving |

## Tips for Accurate Measurements

- Measure the **inner** dimensions of the cargo bed, not the outer
- Account for wheel wells and other obstructions that reduce space
- When loading, material usually piles above the bed edges — our calculator gives the flat-bed volume
- For loose materials like sand/soil, add 10-15% for settling

## Material Weight Reference

While our calculator measures volume, knowing weight helps too:

- Dry sand: ~100 lbs per cubic foot
- Gravel: ~110-120 lbs per cubic foot
- Topsoil: ~80-90 lbs per cubic foot

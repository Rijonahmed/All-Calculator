## Why Understanding Inheritance Matters

When a loved one passes away, understanding how their property should be distributed is crucial. Without proper knowledge, families face conflicts, legal battles, and emotional stress.

Inheritance laws vary significantly by:
- **Country** (Bangladesh, India, USA)
- **Religion** (Muslim, Hindu, Christian)
- **State or region**
- **Presence or absence of a will**

## Inheritance in Bangladesh

Bangladesh follows personal laws based on religion:

### Muslim Inheritance Law

Islamic inheritance (Faraid) is one of the most structured systems:

- **Fixed Shares (Quranic Heirs):** Spouse, parents, and children receive guaranteed portions
- **Residuary Heirs:** Remaining property goes to male agnatic relatives
- **General Rule:** Sons receive twice the share of daughters in the same class

**Example (Muslim Law):**
A man leaves behind a wife, one son, and one daughter:

| Heir | Share |
|------|-------|
| Wife | 1/8 (12.5%) |
| Son | 7/12 (58.3%) |
| Daughter | 7/24 (29.2%) |

### Hindu Inheritance in Bangladesh

- Follows Dayabhaga school of Hindu law
- Property can only be inherited after the owner's death
- Sons and daughters have inheritance rights
- Wife is a Class I heir

### Christian Inheritance in Bangladesh

- Governed by the Succession Act of 1925
- Spouse gets 1/3, children share 2/3 equally
- No gender-based discrimination

## Inheritance in India

### Hindu Succession Act

Under the Hindu Succession Act (amended 2005):

- **Class I Heirs:** Mother, spouse, sons, daughters — all share equally
- **Daughters = Sons:** Daughters have exactly the same rights as sons
- **Per Capita Distribution:** Each heir in the same class gets an equal share

**Example (Hindu Succession Act):**
A man leaves behind his wife, mother, one son, and one daughter. All four are Class I heirs:

| Heir | Share |
|------|-------|
| Wife | 25% |
| Mother | 25% |
| Son | 25% |
| Daughter | 25% |

### Muslim Inheritance in India

- Follows Muslim Personal Law (Shariat)
- Similar to Bangladesh Muslim law
- Fixed shares for Quranic heirs

### Indian Succession Act (Christians)

- Spouse: 1/3 of the estate
- Children: Share the remaining 2/3 equally

## Inheritance in the USA

The USA follows **intestate succession** when there's no will. Laws vary by state, but common patterns include:

### Surviving Spouse + Children
- Spouse typically receives 1/3 to 1/2
- Children split the remainder equally

### No Spouse
- Children inherit equally
- If no children, parents inherit

### No Immediate Family
- Brothers and sisters
- Then grandparents
- Then aunts, uncles, and cousins

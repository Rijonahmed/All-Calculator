## What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple measurement that uses your height and weight to estimate body fat. It's used by doctors, nutritionists, and fitness professionals worldwide as a quick health screening tool.

The formula is simple: **BMI = weight (kg) / height² (m²)**.

## BMI Categories

After calculation, your BMI falls into one of these categories:

| Category | BMI Range |
|----------|-----------|
| Underweight | Below 18.5 |
| Normal Weight | 18.5 - 24.9 |
| Overweight | 25 - 29.9 |
| Obese Class I | 30 - 34.9 |
| Obese Class II | 35 - 39.9 |
| Obese Class III | 40 and above |

## Important Notes

- BMI does not directly measure body fat — athletes with high muscle mass may have a high BMI but low body fat
- BMI should be used as a screening tool, not a diagnostic tool
- Always consult a healthcare provider for a complete health assessment
- BMI ranges may vary slightly for different ethnic groups

## Tips for a Healthy BMI

- Maintain a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains
- Exercise regularly — aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week
- Get adequate sleep (7-9 hours for adults)
- Stay hydrated
- Monitor your weight regularly
